Microbiology and Innate Immunology

The Tang lab is generally interested in understanding the mechanisms of bacterial infectious diseases and developing innovative therapies.

Our primary research interests revolve around utilizing Listeria monocytogenes and Staphylococcus aureus as model strains to investigate antimicrobial resistance, bacterial pathogenesis, and host-microbe interactions mediated by cyclic dinucleotides (CDNs).

The Tang lab is actively recruiting enthusiastic graduate students and postdocs to join the group. Please checkout our research to learn about current research directions.
